When books have a movie to it I feel like I have to pick a side, is the book better or the movie? For Perks of Being a Wallflower I can't pick a side, both the book and the movie are amazing. Of course they sometimes cut out scenes that they think are unimportant or the movie would just be too long. But honestly in my opinion they didn't miss out on too much, when they describe the characters in the book they are exactly like I imagined them when I saw them in the movie. The book/movie showed the struggles that the main character was going through in a really detailed way and it was so emotional (I literally cried 3 times when watching this movie) But this is probably also one of my favorite movies.

Drawing from an angle where you're looking up and the person is looking down is so tricky T-T. It doesn't look great but as long as I tried, I had to draw and re-draw it so many times but im glad I just trusted the process even if it doesn't look ideal.
